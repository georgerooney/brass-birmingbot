# Brass: Birmingham - Rulebook Transcription

## Overview & Goal

The game tells the story of competing entrepreneurs in the West Midlands during the Industrial Revolution.

During the game, you expand your empire by establishing canals and rails, and building/developing various industries.

The game is played over 2 eras: the Canal Era (1770-1830) and the Rail Era (1830-1870).

The winner is the player who has the most Victory Points (VPs) following the end of the Rail Era.

VPs are scored at the end of each era for your Link tiles and flipped Industry tiles.

## Board & Player Setup

### Board Setup

Place the board on either the day or night side.

For player counts less than 4, remove all cards and Merchant tiles that show a player count greater than your number of players and return them to the box.

Place 1 beer barrel on each beer barrel space beside a non-blank Merchant tile.

Place 1 black cube on each space of the Coal Market, leaving 1 of the £1 spaces open.

Place 1 orange cube on each space of the Iron Market, leaving both of the £1 spaces open.

Place remaining cubes and beer barrels beside the board to form a limitless General Supply.

Place the money beside the board to form the Bank.

Separate the Wild Location and Wild Industry cards and place them face up on 2 Card Draw Areas.

Shuffle the remaining cards face down on the remaining Card Draw Area to form the Draw Deck.

Shuffle the remaining Merchant tiles and place 1 face up on each Merchant space marked with the number of players.

In a 2-player game, no Merchant tiles are placed in Warrington and Nottingham.

In a 3-player game, no Merchant tiles are placed in Nottingham.

In a 2-player game, blue and teal coloured Location cards are not in the Draw Deck.

In a 3-player game, teal coloured Location cards are not in the Draw Deck.

Locations removed from the Draw Deck may still be built in.

### Player Setup

Take a Player Mat, £17 from the Bank, and choose a coloured Character tile.

Place matching coloured Link tiles in your player area.

Stack matching coloured Industry tiles on their appropriate slots on your Player Mat, with the black top half face down.

Place your VP Marker on the 0 space of the Progress Track.

Place your Income Marker on the 10 space of the Progress Track.

Draw 8 cards from the Draw Deck for your hidden Hand.

Draw 1 additional card from the Draw Deck and place it face down as your Discard Pile.

Shuffle all players' Character tiles together and place them in a random order on the Turn Order Track.

## Playing the Game

Each era is played in rounds, continuing until both the Draw Deck and players' Hands are exhausted.

There are exactly 8 rounds for 4 players, 9 rounds for 3 players, and 10 rounds for 2 players per era.

On your turn, perform a total of 2 actions.

Exception: During the first round of the Canal Era, each player performs only 1 action.

For each action performed, you must discard a card from your Hand and place it face up on top of your Discard Pile.

Wild cards are instead placed back onto their Card Draw Areas when discarded.

After all your actions are completed, refill your Hand back up to 8 cards from the Draw Deck.

Any money spent during your turn is placed on top of your Character tile on the Turn Order Track.

## Action List

You may choose to perform one of the following actions, and you may perform the same action twice.

### Build Action

Discard an appropriate card face up onto your Discard Pile.

A Location card may be used to build any Industry tile at the named location, even if it is not part of your network.

A Wild Location card may be played as any Location card, except for the 2 Farm Breweries.

An Industry card may be used to build the matching Industry tile in a location that is part of your network.

A Wild Industry card may be played as any Industry card.

Take the lowest level tile of the chosen industry from your Player Mat and place it on an undeveloped space in your chosen location.

Place it on a space displaying only that industry's icon if possible, otherwise on a multi-icon space.

Pay money onto your Character tile on the Turn Order Track.

Consume any required iron and coal.

If building a Coal Mine or Iron Works, place coal or iron from the General Supply onto the tile equal to the quantity shown in the bottom right.

If building a Brewery, place 1 beer barrel on it during the Canal Era, or 2 beer barrels during the Rail Era.

If you build a Coal Mine connected to any Merchant space, or an Iron Works anywhere, you must immediately move as many cubes as possible to available spaces in its associated Market.

Fill the most expensive Market spaces first and collect the corresponding money.

If the last cube is moved to the Market, flip the Industry tile and advance your Income Marker.

If you have no Industry or Link tiles on the board, you may discard an Industry card to build in any undeveloped matching space, or any card to build a Link anywhere.

Farm Breweries can only be built using a Brewery Industry card or a Wild Industry card.

Canal Era: You may have a maximum of 1 Industry tile per location, and cannot build tiles showing a cross-out icon on the Player Mat.

Rail Era: You may build multiple Industry tiles in each location.

#### Overbuilding

You may replace an already placed Industry tile with a higher level tile of the same industry type.

You may Overbuild any of your own Industry tiles, returning any resources on it to the General Supply.

You may Overbuild an opponent's Coal Mine or Iron Works only if there are no resource cubes of that type on the entire board or Market.

Removed overbuilt tiles are returned to the box and do not score VPs, but players keep previously gained income or VPs.

### Sell Action

Discard any card from your Hand.

Choose 1 of your unflipped Cotton Mill, Manufacturer, or Pottery tiles connected to a Merchant tile featuring that industry's icon.

Consume the required amount of beer shown in the top right corner of the tile.

You may consume a beer barrel on the space beside the Merchant tile you are selling to and collect its bonus.

Flip the Industry tile and advance your Income Marker.

You may repeat the process for each of your unflipped Industry tiles in the same action.

Merchant Bonuses:
* Gloucester: Removes 1 lowest level tile for free.
* Oxford: Advances Income 2 spaces.
* Nottingham/Shrewsbury: Advances VP Marker.
* Warrington: Grants £5.

### Loan Action

Discard any card from your Hand.

Take £30 from the Bank.

Move your Income Marker 3 income levels backwards and place it on the highest space within that level.

You cannot take a loan if it drops your income level below -10.

### Develop Action

Discard any card from your Hand.

Remove 1 or 2 of the lowest level Industry tiles from your Player Mat and return them to the box.

Consume 1 iron for each tile removed.

Pottery tiles showing a lightbulb icon may not be developed and must be built to be removed from the mat.

### Scout Action

Discard 3 cards total from your Hand.

Take 1 Wild Location card and 1 Wild Industry card.

You may not perform this action if you already have a Wild card in your Hand.

### Network Action

Discard any card from your Hand.

Place a Link tile on an undeveloped line adjacent to a location that is part of your network.

Canal Era: You may only build canal Links, with a maximum of 1 canal Link per action for £3.

Rail Era: You may only build rail Links. In a single Rail Era Network action, you may build 1 rail Link for £5 and 1 coal, or 2 rail Links for £15, 2 coal, and 1 Brewery beer.

Beer used for a double rail Link must come from a Brewery connected to the second rail Link.

### Passing

You may pass instead of performing an action, but must still discard a card.

## Gameplay Concepts

### Your Network & Connectivity

A location is in your network if it contains your Industry tile or is adjacent to your Link tile.

Locations are connected if you can trace a route of Link tiles owned by any player between them.

### Flipping Tiles & Income

At the end of each era, flipped Industry tiles score VPs.

Sell actions flip Cotton Mills, Manufacturers, and Potteries.

Coal Mines, Iron Works, and Breweries flip when the last resource is removed.

When a tile flips, immediately advance your Income Marker by the spaces shown on the tile.

### Consuming Resources

Coal: Must be consumed from the closest connected unflipped Coal Mine. If none, purchase from the Coal Market if connected to a market icon.

Iron: Must be consumed from any unflipped Iron Works. No connection needed. If none, purchase from the Iron Market.

Beer: Consume from your own unflipped Breweries (no connection needed), an opponent's Brewery (must be connected), or the Merchant space you are selling to.

## End of Round Maintenance

Determine turn order: The player who spent the least money goes first, and the most money goes last. Ties in spent money keep their relative turn order.

Return all money from Character tiles to the Bank.

Take income: Take money from the Bank equal to your income level.

If income is negative, pay the Bank.

If you have a shortfall, remove an Industry tile from the board to gain half its cost.

If you have no tiles to remove, lose 1 VP for each £1 you are short.

## End of Era & Game

An era ends after the round where all players use their final cards.

Score Link tiles: 1 VP for each yellow hex icon in adjacent locations, then remove the Link tiles.

Score flipped Industry tiles: Based on the VP value in the bottom left corner. Unflipped tiles do not score.

End of Canal Era: Remove all level 1 Industry tiles from the board. Reset Merchant beer on empty non-blank spaces. Shuffle Discard Piles and Draw Deck together, and draw new hands of 8 cards.

Winning: The player with the most VPs after the Rail Era scoring wins.

Ties are broken by highest income, then most remaining money.

## Introductory Game Variant

Play only the Canal Era.

Score standard Canal Era points, plus:

Score 1 VP for every £10 (up to 15 VPs).

Score VPs equal to your income level (lose VPs if negative).

Score your level 2 or greater Industry tiles a second time.
